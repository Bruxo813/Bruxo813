Benefits of Microdosing

Microdosing with psilocybin has been shown to have a range of potential benefits, including increased creativity, improved mood, and reduced anxiety.

  • Improved Mood: Research suggests that microdosing can lead to a more positive mood, increased emotional stability, and reduced symptoms of depression.
  • Enhanced Creativity: Many users report a boost in creativity and problem-solving skills, making it easier to think outside the box.
  • Increased Focus: Microdosing can help improve concentration and productivity, making it a popular choice for professionals and students.
  • Reduced Anxiety: Some studies indicate that microdosing can help reduce anxiety and stress, providing a sense of calm and well-being.
  • Improved Relationships: Users often experience improved empathy and communication, enhancing personal and professional relationships.

Understanding Psilocybe Cubensis

Psilocybe cubensis is a species of psychedelic mushroom that contains the psychoactive compounds psilocybin and psilocin. It is one of the most commonly cultivated and consumed magic mushrooms due to its relative ease of growth and moderate potency. P. cubensis mushrooms are often found in subtropical and tropical regions of the world, particularly in Central and South America, Southeast Asia, and parts of Australia.

Dosage Information

The effects of Psilocybe cubensis mushrooms can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity, mushroom potency, and the specific conditions under which the mushrooms were cultivated. Here is a general guideline for dosage levels, but please note that these can vary:

  • Microdose (sub-perceptual effects): 0.1 to 0.3 grams of dried mushrooms
  • Low dose (mild effects): 0.5 to 1 gram of dried mushrooms
  • Moderate dose (typical effects): 1 to 2.5 grams of dried mushrooms
  • High dose (strong effects): 2.5 to 5 grams of dried mushrooms
  • Heroic dose (very intense effects): 5 grams or more of dried mushrooms

For first-time users, it is recommended to start with a low dose to gauge sensitivity and avoid any overwhelming effects. Keep in mind that the potency of mushrooms can vary, so it's essential to use caution and adjust the dosage accordingly.

It is important to remember that the possession, distribution, and cultivation of psilocybin-containing mushrooms, including Psilocybe cubensis, are illegal in many jurisdictions. Engaging in these activities may have legal consequences. Additionally, consuming these mushrooms can have unpredictable effects and may not be suitable for everyone. Always exercise caution and consult with a healthcare professional if considering the use of psychedelics for therapeutic purposes.

How much should I take?

Microdosing involves taking small, sub-perceptual doses of a psychedelic substance, such as psilocybin, with the intent of experiencing subtle, non-hallucinogenic effects. The doses are typically around 1/10th to 1/20th of a recreational dose, and users often follow a schedule, such as once every three days, to maintain the potential benefits.

When microdosing psilocybin, it is important to follow a schedule that allows for rest days between doses to minimize the risk of tolerance build-up and potential side effects. One commonly followed schedule is the "Fadiman protocol," named after Dr. James Fadiman, a researcher and advocate of psychedelic therapy.

The Fadiman protocol suggests taking a microdose once every three days, with the following pattern:

  • Day 1: Take a microdose (typically around 0.1 to 0.3 grams of dried Psilocybe cubensis mushrooms or an equivalent amount for other species)
  • Day 2: Rest (no microdose)
  • Day 3: Rest (no microdose)
  • Day 4: Take the next microdose and repeat the cycle

By spacing out the microdoses, users can potentially maximize the benefits while reducing the risk of developing tolerance or experiencing adverse effects. However, it's essential to listen to your body and adjust the dosing schedule as needed.

Effects of microdosing psilocybin may include:

  • Enhanced creativity: Some users report increased creative thinking, problem-solving, and openness to new ideas while microdosing.
  • Improved mood: Microdosing may help alleviate symptoms of depression or anxiety by promoting a positive mood and emotional well-being.
  • Increased focus and productivity: Users often report improved concentration and a heightened ability to engage in tasks requiring sustained attention.
  • Greater self-awareness and introspection: Microdosing may facilitate self-reflection and personal growth, allowing users to gain a better understanding of their thoughts and emotions.

Keep in mind that research on microdosing is still limited, and long-term effects and safety are not well-established. Additionally, the use of psilocybin, even in small amounts, remains illegal in many jurisdictions and may carry risks, such as adverse reactions or potential interactions with other medications. Always exercise caution and consult with a healthcare professional if you are considering microdosing for therapeutic purposes.

Microdosing, is it safe?

In November 2020, Oregon passed Measure 109, which made it the first state in the United States to legalize the use of psilocybin in a controlled, therapeutic setting. This does not mean that psilocybin is legalized for recreational use or for unsupervised therapeutic purposes. Instead, the measure establishes a program for the supervised administration of psilocybin by licensed practitioners to adults aged 21 and older.

The benefits of microdosing, or taking a full therapeutic dose of psilocybin, have been researched in recent years, and several potential benefits have been identified:

  • Treatment of depression: Studies have shown that psilocybin therapy can have rapid and long-lasting effects in reducing depressive symptoms, particularly in patients with treatment-resistant depression.
  • Anxiety reduction:Psilocybin has shown promise in reducing anxiety levels in patients with life-threatening illnesses or chronic anxiety disorders.
  • Treatment of addiction: Preliminary research suggests that psilocybin may help individuals struggling with substance abuse, such as alcohol or tobacco addiction, by altering their thought patterns and behaviors.
  • Personal growth and self-discovery: Many users report meaningful and transformative experiences during psilocybin sessions, which can lead to lasting changes in their outlook on life, values, and relationships.
  • Improvement in overall well-being: Some studies have shown that psilocybin can lead to increased feelings of well-being and life satisfaction.

It is essential to note that while these benefits have been observed in clinical studies, psilocybin therapy may not be suitable for everyone. The effects of psilocybin can vary depending on the individual, and there are potential risks and adverse effects associated with its use.

In Oregon, the psilocybin therapy program is being developed under the guidance of the Oregon Health Authority, with a focus on safety, accessibility, and adherence to evidence-based practices.
